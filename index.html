Today's Condition
—
The distance between you and your teen isn't failure. It's the doorway.
In the moment →
Before You Reach Them
I'm Activated
You can't regulate your teen until you regulate yourself. Start here — even 60 seconds helps.
Breathe in...
Follow the circle
1
Notice where you feel this in your body. Name it without judgment — chest, jaw, shoulders.
2
Name 5 things you can see right now. Slow down. Really look at each one.
3
Keep breathing until there's even a small shift. You don't need to be calm — just slightly less reactive.
4
When you're ready: be near your teen. No agenda. No fixing. Your presence is the intervention.
Your nervous system is the intervention. Regulation before relationship.
Attunement
My Teen Is Shut Down
Shutdown is a nervous system response, not a choice. Your teen isn't pushing you away — they're protecting themselves. Your job right now is presence, not pursuit.
Right now
Stay nearby. Not hovering — just close. Your regulated, quiet presence is the intervention.
Resist the urge to
Draw them out. Fix it. Fill the silence. Process what happened. Silence doesn't need solving — it needs space.
When they surface
Stay low-key. "I'm here" is enough. Keep your tone warm and your agenda invisible.
Something that often helps
Do something ordinary side by side — no eye contact required. Parallel presence says "I'm with you" without pressure.
They are not rejecting you. They are regulating. Your steadiness is what makes it safe to come back.
The R.E.A.L. Method
We Had a Rupture
Ruptures aren't the failure. Unrepaired ruptures are. Four steps, in order.
R
Regulate the Breath
Before anything else, pause. Three to five slow breaths. Feet on the ground. Soften your jaw. Their nervous system is watching yours — settle yours first.
E
Explore the Moment
Lead with curiosity, not correction. Don't defend, fix, or explain why they're wrong.
"What was going on for you?"
A
Attune with Empathy
Reflect what you heard. Understanding isn't agreement — it's the signal that they're not alone in this.
"It sounds like you felt ___ when I ___."
L
Lean Back In
The gesture of return. Not a lecture, not a solution — just the signal: I'm still here. We're still connected.
"I messed up. I'm sorry. Can we start over?"
Securely attached kids don't have perfect parents. They have parents who repair.
Language of Repair
I Want to Repair
Repair isn't about being right. It's about restoring connection. These micro-shifts change everything.
✗ "You need to calm down."
✓ "I can see you're really upset right now."
✗ "Why do you always do this?"
✓ "Help me understand what happened."
✗ "You started it."
✓ "Let me take responsibility for my part."
✗ "You're being dramatic."
✓ "Your feelings make sense to me."
✗ "I'm done talking about this."
✓ "I need a few minutes. I'll come back."
The goal isn't a perfect conversation. It's a repaired one.
Expressed Delight
Things Are Good
Good moments are worth tending. Connection deepens when delight is expressed — not just felt internally.
Name it
What is one thing about your teen right now that genuinely moves you? Not an achievement — something about who they are.
Let it show
When did you last let your teen see that their existence delights you? A look, a laugh, a pause to really notice them.
Say something
Delight expressed out loud wires something in a teen's brain: I am wanted. My presence brings light. They need to hear it, not just sense it.
One thing today
What could you say or do in the next 24 hours that would tell your teen: you bring me joy, just by being you?
The good moments are the practice too. Tend them.
